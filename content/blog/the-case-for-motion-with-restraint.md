Motion earns its place when it helps users understand what changed, what matters, and where to go next.

## Treat animation as hierarchy

Entrance timing, staggered reveals, and layered depth can create useful structure. The key is restraint. If everything moves, nothing feels intentional.

## Respect reduced motion

Every motion system should degrade gracefully. Accessibility is not a post-processing pass. It changes how animation is introduced from the start.

## Use motion to strengthen quality perception

Interfaces that move well often feel more considered, but only when the underlying layout, spacing, and interaction model are already solid.
