The pace that impresses clients or hiring managers is rarely raw speed. It is structured speed with fewer reversals, smaller handoffs, and better defaults.

## Build a narrow first version

When a team says they need to move faster, they usually have a scope problem before they have a velocity problem. The first intervention is to cut the initial surface area until it becomes obvious what can ship first.

## Design and engineering should converge early

A lot of delivery drag shows up when design and implementation happen in long disconnected phases. The fix is not more meetings. The fix is moving critical layout, component, and content decisions earlier.

## Remove decision debt

Every unresolved choice compounds later. Naming, loading patterns, form validation, data contracts, and component ownership all become hidden tax if they are left ambiguous.

A strong delivery system makes those decisions once, documents them, and reuses them.
